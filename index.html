MAY 2025! issue: 1

Dear colleagues,

As part of our continued commitment to driving better health outcomes and shaping the future of healthcare across public and private sectors, various teams have represented the organization at several high-impact conferences. These platforms have not only showcased our leadership in key therapeutic areas i.e. obesity, diabetes and rare diseases but also helped us to engage with stakeholders, drive important policy conversations, and reinforce our purpose of improving lives.
BHF Conference | Catalysing Policy Conversations on Obesity!
Held in Cape Town from 10–14 May, the 24th Annual BHF Conference drew over 1,000 health funders and policymakers. We proudly sponsored Prof. Partha Kar (NHS England) to speak on obesity as a complex and chronic disease — a message that strongly resonated with attendees. Our session, Holistic Management of Obesity, delivered by Prof. Kar, marked a key milestone in shifting the narrative: reframing obesity as both a chronic disease and a modifiable risk factor. This platform also created a valuable opportunity to begin critical conversations with funders about the long-term burden of obesity on medical schemes — and the importance of value-based solutions. To reinforce our presence, our obesity-focused advert was featured in the BHF magazine, extending the impact of our message beyond the stage. Following Prof. Kar’s presentation, we supported awareness efforts further by distributing desk drops highlighting South African obesity statistics, helping to engage stakeholders and inform policy makers.
Showcasing Leadership at SEMSD
Our team is proud to be attending the South African Endocrine and Metabolic Society for Diabetes in Africa (SEMSDA) congress this year, reinforcing our commitment to advancing care in diabetes and metabolic health. As part of our support, we have proudly sponsored three expert speakers who will contribute to critical conversations shaping the future of diabetes management on the continent.
ECO 2025 | European Congress on Obesity
Our colleagues attended ECO 2025 in Spain, joining global leaders to explore cutting-edge data and strategies in obesity prevention and treatment. The congress highlighted new outcomes data, precision-based approaches to obesity care, and digital tools driving adherence.

These global insights are essential as we work to localise innovation and support evidence-based policy shifts in South Africa and the broader region.
FCPSA Conference | A Strategic Lens on Diabetes and Obesity
Held from 2–4 May in Cape Town, the Faculty of Consulting Physicians of South Africa (FCPSA) Conference featured key sessions on the evolving approach to cardiometabolic care. With over 200 clinicians attending, this platform emphasized the importance of aligning outcomes between patients, clinicians, and funders.

Key insights:
• New guidelines advocating waist-to-height ratio over BMI for obesity classification.
• STEP trial learnings on semaglutide.
• Real-world perspectives on long-term weight management and treatment sustainability.
SAAHIP Conference |
Public Sector Reflections
and Momentum

The SAAHIP Conference 2025 gave Public Sector colleagues an opportunity to engage in dynamic plenaries and reaffirm our partnership with healthcare professionals in the state sector. Key themes included pharmacy's role in universal health coverage, practical solutions to medicine access challenges, and the growing interest in public-private collaborations.
GGPF | Primary Care and Public Health in Focus

Held on the 13th of April in Gauteng – the event was attended by approximately 250 doctors. GGPF conferences have become a cornerstone for Family Practitioners seeking to stay abreast of the latest industry trends and ensuring compliance with HPCSA regulations on continuous education. The team shared insights and innovations at the Gauteng General Practitioners Forum (GGPF) event—continuing our collaboration with primary care leaders and highlighting our solutions for chronic disease management in community settings.
Case Managers South Africa (CMASA) Congress

Held from 2–5 April in Umhlanga, this congress brought together 200 delegates across private and public funders. Michel Nel's session on obesity as a modifiable risk factor drew strong engagement, reinforcing the need for proactive management. Interest in obesity, diabetes, and rare diseases was notable, with follow-up discussions underway for a potential pilot with CAMAF. Key highlights included strong recognition of obesity's cost impact on medical schemes, growing funder openness to covering treatments like Ozempic, and excitement around the upcoming launch of NovoEight in rare diseases.
